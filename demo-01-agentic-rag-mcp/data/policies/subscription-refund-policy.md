# Subscription Refund Policy

This policy governs refunds for recurring subscription charges. Subscriptions differ from one-time orders, and this policy overrides the standard refund policy where the two conflict.

## Refund Window: 7 Days (Not 30)

For subscriptions, the refund window is **7 days** from the date each recurring charge is billed. This **explicitly contradicts the 30-day window in the standard refund policy** — that 30-day window does **not** apply to subscription charges. A subscription charge older than 7 days is outside the refund window and is handled as cancellation only.

## Pro-Rata Calculation

When a subscription refund is approved within the 7-day window, calculate the refund pro-rata based on the unused portion of the billing period:

1. Determine the length of the billing period (for example, 30 days for a monthly plan).
2. Count the days already elapsed since the charge.
3. Refund the proportion of the charge corresponding to the unused days.

For example, if a monthly subscriber cancels 3 days into a 30-day period, the pro-rata refund covers the remaining 27 days.

## Cancellation vs. Refund

These are distinct actions:

- **Cancellation** stops future renewals. It does not return money already charged.
- **Refund** returns money for the current period and is only available within the 7-day window.

A customer may cancel at any time, but a refund requires the request to fall within the 7-day window. Always clarify which outcome the customer wants. Refunds may never exceed the amount of the original subscription charge.
