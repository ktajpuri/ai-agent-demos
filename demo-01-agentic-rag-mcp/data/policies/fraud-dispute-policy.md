# Fraud and Dispute Policy

This policy covers cases where a customer or card issuer challenges a charge as unauthorized or fraudulent. These cases are handled as **disputes** and **chargebacks**, which follow a different process from ordinary order adjustments.

## Disputes vs. Chargebacks

A **dispute** is raised when a customer contests a charge with us directly, claiming it was unauthorized, duplicated, or not recognized. A **chargeback** is raised when the customer's card issuer formally reverses a charge on the customer's behalf. A dispute may escalate into a chargeback if it is not resolved.

## Filing a Dispute

When a customer reports an unauthorized or unrecognized charge:

1. Verify the customer's identity and locate the transaction.
2. Open a dispute case and record the disputed amount and reason.
3. Place a hold on the order to prevent further action.
4. Gather supporting evidence: transaction logs, delivery confirmation, and account activity.

## Timelines

Disputes must be acknowledged within 24 hours of being reported. A chargeback initiated by a card issuer carries a strict response deadline set by the card network — typically a number of days during which we must submit evidence. Missing the chargeback response deadline results in an automatic reversal in the customer's favor.

## Resolution

A dispute is resolved when we either accept the claim and reverse the charge through the dispute channel, or decline it with supporting evidence. A chargeback is resolved by the card network based on the evidence both sides submit. Record the outcome and the case reference on the order.

For unauthorized-charge cases, always handle them as disputes or chargebacks — never through the ordinary order-adjustment channel.
