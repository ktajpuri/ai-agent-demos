# Escalation Paths

This policy defines when a support agent must escalate a case, who handles escalations, expected response times, and what information to include.

## When to Escalate

Escalate a case when:

- A refund would exceed the 50% partial cap or otherwise fall outside an agent's authority.
- A refund is requested after the 30-day standard window has closed.
- A customer disputes whether a prior refund was received.
- The original payment method is invalid and an alternative disbursement is needed.
- A fraud dispute or chargeback requires evidence gathering beyond routine handling.
- A multi-currency or amount-limit edge case cannot be resolved with standard rules.

## Who Handles Escalations

- **Tier 1 (Agent):** Routine refunds within standard limits.
- **Tier 2 (Supervisor):** Out-of-window exceptions, above-cap partial refunds, payment-method changes.
- **Tier 3 (Specialist team):** Fraud disputes, chargebacks, and complex multi-currency cases.

Escalate to the lowest tier that has authority to resolve the case.

## Response Times

- Tier 2 supervisor review: within 1 business day.
- Tier 3 specialist review: within 2 business days.
- Fraud disputes: acknowledged within 24 hours given chargeback deadlines.

## What Information to Include

Every escalation must include: the order reference, the customer's verified identity, the requested action and amount, the policy that applies, the reason it exceeds agent authority, and any supporting evidence. Incomplete escalations are returned to the agent and delay resolution. Never promise the customer an outcome before the escalation is approved.
