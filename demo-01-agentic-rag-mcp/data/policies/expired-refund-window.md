# Expired Refund Window

This policy applies when a customer requests a refund after the standard 30-day refund window has passed.

## Default Position

After 30 days from the order date, an order is **no longer eligible for a standard refund**. Agents should not process a standard refund for an order outside the window without an approved exception.

## Limited Exceptions

A small number of exceptions may justify a refund after the window closes:

- A documented product defect that was reported within the window but not yet resolved.
- A billing error attributable to the company.
- A service outage or non-delivery confirmed in our records.
- A legal or regulatory requirement in the customer's jurisdiction.

Exceptions are not granted simply because the customer was unaware of the deadline. Each exception must be documented with the supporting reason.

## Escalation Path

Refunds outside the window require supervisor approval. The agent should:

1. Confirm the order is genuinely past the 30-day window.
2. Gather evidence supporting the requested exception.
3. Escalate to a supervisor with the order details and evidence.
4. Wait for an approval decision before promising anything to the customer.

See the escalation paths policy for response times and required information.

## How to Communicate to the Customer

Be empathetic but clear. Tell the customer the standard window has passed, that you are reviewing whether an exception applies, and that a supervisor must approve any out-of-window refund. Do not guarantee a refund before approval. If the exception is denied, explain the decision plainly and offer any alternative assistance available.
