# Partial Refund Policy

A partial refund returns a portion of the order total rather than the full amount. This policy explains when partial refunds are allowed, how the amount is determined, and who must approve them.

## When Partial Refunds Are Allowed

Partial refunds may be issued when:

- A customer keeps part of an order but is dissatisfied with a portion of it.
- A goodwill adjustment is warranted (for example, a delivery delay or minor damage).
- A discount or price-match correction applies after purchase.

Partial refunds are not used for full returns; those follow the standard refund policy.

## Maximum Amount

A partial refund is **capped at 50% of the order value**. Under no circumstances should a single partial refund exceed half of what the customer originally paid for the order. If a customer's situation appears to justify more than 50%, the request must be escalated rather than approved as a partial refund.

## How the Amount Is Calculated

1. Start from the total amount the customer paid for the order.
2. Determine the adjustment warranted by the situation.
3. Cap the adjustment at 50% of the order value.
4. The resulting figure is the partial refund amount.

## Approval Process

Partial refunds up to the 50% cap may be approved by a support agent. Any partial refund that would exceed the 50% cap requires supervisor approval through the escalation path. Always record the reason for the partial refund and the calculation used on the order notes.

Refunds may never exceed the original order amount; see the refund amount limits policy.
