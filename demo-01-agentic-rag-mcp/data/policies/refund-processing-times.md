# Refund Processing Times

This policy explains how long refunds take, how to communicate timelines to customers, and what to do when a refund is delayed.

## Processing Times by Type

Processing time depends on the payment method and the refund type:

- **Credit or debit card:** typically 5–10 business days for the refund to appear on the customer's statement after we issue it.
- **Digital wallet:** typically 1–3 business days.
- **Bank transfer / direct debit:** typically 3–7 business days.
- **Subscription pro-rata refunds:** issued immediately on approval but subject to the same payment-method appearance times above.
- **Fraud disputes and chargebacks:** governed by the card network's timeline, which is separate and often longer.

These times begin when we successfully issue the refund, not when the customer first requests it.

## How to Communicate Timelines

Always give the customer a realistic range, not a single day. State that the timeline begins once the refund is issued and that the final appearance depends on their bank or provider. Provide the refund reference number so the customer can follow up with their provider if needed.

## What to Do If Delayed

If a refund has not appeared after the upper end of the expected range:

1. Confirm the refund was actually issued and locate its reference number.
2. Check whether the original payment method is still valid.
3. Ask the customer to confirm with their bank, as appearance times can vary.
4. If the refund was issued but cannot be traced after the expected window, escalate so the disbursement can be investigated.

Never issue a second refund to resolve a delay; see the already-refunded orders policy.
